# CRESCENT 新月牌

*Official Rulebook*

---

## 1. Objective

Crescent is a competitive card game for **2 players**. Each game consists of **3 rounds**. The first player to win 2 rounds wins the game. If a round ends in a tie, both players are considered to have lost that round.

## 2. Components

### 2.1 Card Types

The game contains the following card types:

- **Element** (22 cards) — Point-scoring cards placed in the Element Zone.
- **Strike** (18 cards) — Combat cards placed in the Strike Zone. May trigger Awaken.
- **Support** (18 cards) — Utility cards placed in the Support Zone.
- **Spell** (20 cards) — Effect cards that resolve immediately, then enter the Graveyard.
- **Hero** — Each player selects one Hero to represent them for the game.
- **Divine Boon** — Special cards granted by mythological patrons. Set aside at the start of the game.
- **Awakened** — Powerful cards that replace a complete Strike Series upon Awakening. Set aside at the start of the game.
- **Token Spell** — Derived Spell cards generated by specific Hero abilities.

The four main card types (Element, Strike, Support, Spell) form the **main deck** of 78 cards.

### 2.2 Other Components

- **Hero Energy Track** — One per player, used to track energy.
- **Awakened Cards** — Set aside before the game begins.
- **Divine Boon Deck** — Shuffled once at the start of the game and kept in a fixed order thereafter.

## 3. Setup

**Constructed Mode / Standard Mode:**

Shuffle all 78 main deck cards and distribute as follows:

| Component | Count |
|---|---|
| Starting hand (per player) | 12 cards |
| Personal deck (per player) | 14 cards |
| Market (shared) | 26 cards |

Place all Hero cards face-down. Players alternate drafting Heroes, selecting 2 each. After viewing their starting hand, each player chooses 1 Hero as their representative for the game. Place the chosen Hero in the Hero Zone with energy set to the Hero's starting value.

## 4. Game Flow

### 4.1 Round Structure

Each game consists of up to 3 rounds. Within each round, players alternate taking turns and playing cards. When both players consecutively pass (decline to play further), the round ends. The player with the higher total Power on the field wins the round.

### 4.2 General Rules

- There is **no hand size limit**.
- Your Hero's energy has a **maximum of 10** and a **minimum of 0**. If an effect would reduce your energy below 0, it remains at 0.
- When a round ends, all cards on the field are sent to their respective owners' Graveyards.

## 5. The Market

The Market displays **4 face-up cards** at all times. At the start of your turn, you may purchase any number of cards from the Market at a cost of **3 energy per card**. After your purchasing phase concludes, refill the Market to 4 cards from the Market deck.

## 6. Round Compensation

| Round | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Round 1** | Determine the starting player by rock-paper-scissors. The winner decides who plays first. |
| **Round 2** | The Round 1 winner draws 1 card from their personal deck. The Round 1 loser plays first. |
| **Round 3** | Both players draw 1 card from their personal deck. The Round 2 winner draws 1 additional card. The Round 2 loser plays first. |

## 7. Turn Structure

On each turn, follow these steps in order:

1. **Pass Check** — Decide whether to pass (end your participation in this round).
2. **Market Phase** — Decide whether to purchase cards from the Market.
3. **Play Phase** — Play 1 card from your hand (unless otherwise specified).

> Once your opponent has passed, you may either pass immediately to end the round, or continue playing additional turns until you choose to pass.

## 8. Field Zones

Each player's field is divided into the following zones:

| Zone | Description |
|---|---|
| **Element Zone** | Where Element cards are placed. Power stacks incrementally: the 1st card contributes 5, the 2nd contributes 10, the 3rd contributes 15, and the 4th contributes 20. The default stacking cap is 20 (i.e., a maximum of 4 Element cards). |
| **Strike Zone** | Where Strike cards are placed. Each Strike card has a fixed Power of **10**. |
| **Support Zone** | Where Support cards are placed. Each Support card has a fixed Power of **5**. |
| **Hero Zone** | Where your chosen Hero is placed. |

Spell cards are not placed on the field; they resolve immediately upon being played and are then sent to the Graveyard. Divine Boon cards function similarly to Spell cards but are **not** considered Spell cards (see [Section 10](#10-divine-boon-rules)).

## 9. Keyword Reference

| Keyword | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Awaken** | When you control 3 cards of the same Strike Series in your Strike Zone, send all 3 (including any Enchanted cards such as Moonward) to your Graveyard and place the corresponding Awakened card in your Strike Zone. Then resolve the Awakened card's Awaken effect. Each Series may Awaken at most twice per round. |
| **Surge X** | When you play a card with Surge, you may choose to pay X energy to trigger the Surge effect. If you do not have enough energy, the Surge effect cannot be activated. |
| **Scry X** | Look at the top X cards of your deck. You may place them on the top or bottom of your deck in any order. |
| **Shroud** | A card with Shroud cannot be destroyed, moved, or returned to hand. It is not considered the highest or lowest Power card for targeting purposes. Enchanting a Shrouded card and returning it to the field are not affected. |
| **Vanish** | When this card would be sent to the Graveyard, exile it (remove it from the game) instead. A card with Vanish cannot be returned to hand. |
| **Enchant** | Play this card attached to another card. A card may only be Enchanted once at a time. (Twinspell is played simultaneously with its target and does not consume your play for the turn. Moonward does consume your play.) |
| **Destroy** | Send the targeted card to its owner's Graveyard. |
| **Return** | Move a card from the field back to its owner's hand. |
| **Move** | Transfer a card from one player's field to the other player's corresponding zone. |
| **Choose one** | Select one of the listed options to resolve. (If Luna is in play on your side, resolve all options instead.) |
| **Series** | A named group of related cards. The following are each considered a Series: each Strike archetype (Desolation, Miku, Shiranui), each Element sub-type (Sprite, Crystal), and a Twinspell copy paired with its original. Cards with the same name but different IDs are NOT a Series. |

## 10. Divine Boon Rules

- Divine Boon cards are played in the same manner as Spell cards and count as your play for the turn.
- Effects that target Spell cards (e.g., Hush, Lilia's Hero ability) do **not** affect Divine Boon cards.
- Divine Boon cards are **not** considered part of your hand. They cannot be discarded or exchanged by your opponent's effects.
- After use, a Divine Boon card is immediately exiled and placed at the bottom of the Divine Boon deck.
- The Divine Boon deck is shuffled once at the start of each game and remains in that fixed order thereafter.

## 11. Twinspell Rules

- When Twinspell Enchants a card with Shroud, the Twinspell also gains Shroud.
- When Twinspell Enchants a Strike Series card that triggers an Awaken, the Twinspell is exiled along with the original cards.
- When a single-target Destroy or Return effect targets a card Enchanted by Twinspell, the Twinspell must be removed or returned **first**.
- A single-target Move effect cannot target Twinspell alone. If you Move the Enchanted card, Twinspell moves with it. This forced movement does **not** apply to single-target Destroy or Return effects.
- The Enchanted card's Surge effect requires a separate Surge decision for each resolution.
- Twinspell is played simultaneously with its target and does **not** consume your play for the turn.
- When Twinspell enters the Graveyard, it is treated as a Spell card.

## 12. Additional Card Rulings

### 12.1 Silence

Silence's timing window occurs **before** a Spell card resolves, including before any Surge decision on the Spell. After you decline to use Silence, your opponent may then choose whether to Surge. Silence may still be activated after you have passed for the round.

### 12.2 Triplecast and Twinspell

When Triplecast or Twinspell is played, it may trigger Lilia's Hero ability. On the 2nd and 3rd resolution (Triplecast), a separate Surge decision is required each time. However, repeated resolutions do **not** re-trigger Lilia's ability. (Repeated resolution is not considered a new play.)

### 12.3 Prismatic Pudding

When Prismatic Pudding enters the field, you must declare which Element Series it belongs to (Sprite or Crystal). Whenever it is moved, returned to hand, or placed onto the field from the Graveyard, you may re-declare its Series.

## 13. Hero-Specific Rulings

### 13.1 Riffbreaker

When a round ends and Element cards are sent to the Graveyard, this does **not** trigger Riffbreaker's ability. However, during each of your opponent's turns after you have passed, Riffbreaker's ability may still trigger.

### 13.2 Shortcake

Shortcake's ability triggers whenever Shortcake's controller is the first to pass in any round, as long as the overall game has not yet concluded. This includes scenarios where the player has no remaining hand cards after Round 2.
